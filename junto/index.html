A Society for Intellectual Development and Practical Judgment

Purpose

The Junto exists to cultivate clear thinking, sound judgment, self-improvement among its members. It is a club dedicated to serious discussion across philosophy, current events, careers, business, health, and productivity with an emphasis on being actionable and applicable to real life projects. The aim is not debate for its own sake, but for the steady development of members who think rigorously, act deliberately, and lead effectively.

Membership

Membership is selective and voluntary.

Members are expected to:

  • Show up consistently
  • Prepare for discussion
  • Engage in good faith
  • Contribute thoughtfully and respectfully

The society is intentionally small to preserve depth of discussion. Membership is extended by invitation only.

Meetings

The Junto meets every other Wednesday evening for 90 minutes, 1800-1930.

Each meeting follows a consistent structure:

  1. Brief update - personal or intellectual update.
  2. Main discussion - a pre-announced topic within a set domain. Share notes and insights.
  3. Application round - how the discussion should change beliefs, behavior, or decisions.
  4. Close - assignment of the next topic. Topics will usually come with an assigned reading.

"What is one belief I'm updating or one behavior I'm changing?"

Domains of Discussion

The Junto rotates across several standing domains:

  • Philosophy and ethics
  • Current events over 1 month old and public affairs
  • Careers and long-term professional development
  • Business and value creation
  • Health, energy, and personal effectiveness

Topics are selected to encourage first-principles thinking, not reactive opinion or news consumption.

Each meeting:

  • One domain
  • One framing question
  • One lead facilitator

Norms and Conduct

Members are expected to:

  • Argue ideas, not personalities
  • Seek understanding before critique
  • Avoid performative debate
  • Speak honestly about uncertainty and error
  • No phone use

The point is to find the truth, not to win an argument. Be curious.

Preparation Rules

Every participant must bring one (1) of the following:

  • A short written note (3–5 bullets)
  • A model (diagram, principle, analogy)
  • A personal example (success or failure)

Continuity

Leadership and facilitation rotate among members. The society is designed to outlast any individual participant through shared norms, consistent structure, and collective ownership.

"When men differ in opinion, both sides ought equally to distrust themselves."

— Benjamin Franklin

The Four Questions

  1. What is true?
  2. Why does it matter?
  3. Where is this misunderstood?
  4. How should it change how I act?
