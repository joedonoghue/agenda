The Case for Skill Stacking

The math of percentiles is not linear.

1% is often a lifestyle. 10% is often a project.

Going from 50% → 90% in a skill can happen fast with deliberate practice. Going from 90% → 99% is where the time curve explodes — tiny gains, rare mentorship, elite environments.

The trade-off:

  • Top 1% requires near-total investment with diminishing returns
  • Top 10% in two complementary skills can be achieved with disciplined but realistic effort
  • The "bridge" person who closes loops between domains is often scarce

What Multiplies Your Skills

Things that multiply reach:

  • Platforms — YouTube, publishing, social media, podcasts
  • Technical skills — web development, automation, data analysis
  • Audience and network effects — every follower amplifies future work
  • AI tools — writing assistance, research, production quality

AI makes these cheaper:

  • First-draft writing, basic slides
  • Routine coding snippets
  • Straightforward analysis and summaries
  • "Google-able" knowledge work

Things that stay scarce:

  • Taste and judgment — knowing what's worth doing and what "good" looks like
  • Trust and reputation — built over years, not manufactured
  • Original thinking and synthesis — connecting dots others don't see
  • Ownership — being the person accountable for outcomes
  • Context and constraints — legal, security, organizational reality

  • Healthcare + Video/YouTube = trusted medical education with real credibility
  • Music + Web development = direct-to-fan platforms, own your distribution
  • History + Sculpture = physical artifacts that teach and endure
  • Hunting/outdoor + Entrepreneurship = lifestyle brand with authentic credibility
  • Writing + Politics = influence through ideas, not just commentary

Questions for Thought

  • Which of your skills is worth a lifestyle commitment? Which is worth a 90-day sprint?
  • What two skills, combined, would make you hard to replace or easy to remember?
  • What "proof-of-work artifact" would demonstrate your unique combination?
  • How do you avoid being the person who's "pretty good at many things" but owns nothing?
  • What platform or tool could multiply your existing credibility?

The goal is not to know a little about everything. It's to become the person who can close loops that otherwise require two or three people.
